Music Biography

My first experience with music was in elementary school where I learned how to play the recorder and sang in the chorus as a soprano. When I entered junior high school, I ventured away from music seeing what other options were open for me in other school activites. The summer before eighth grade, I started playing guitar in a rock band at a summer camp. After the summer camp, I continued to study guitar with Benjamin Gomez. I studied various styles of playing ranging from classical, flamenco, jazz, and some forms of metal.

Once I entered high school, I started playing upright bass while studying with Benjamin Gomez. I enrolled in the Stockdale High School String Ensemble and Symphony Orchestra, while continuing my studies in jazz guitar by playing in the Stockdale High School Jazz Band. Outside of school activities, I began singing with the St. Phillip's the Apostle, Joyful Noise choir as an alto my freshman year. My sophmore year, I began to play with the Bakersfield Youth Symphony Orchestra and started playing electric bass for the Joyful Noise choir. I still continued with the Stockdale High School's string ensemble, symphony orchestra, and jazz band. In my senior year, I began studying with Whitney Herbst and participated in the Kern County's Honor Orchestra program and Stockdale High School Wind Symphony while playing in the previous ensembles listed. I performed in the CMEA Solo/Ensemble Frestival in Bakersfield, California playing The Elephant by Camille Saint-Seans and Piano Quintet in A Major, D. 667 by Franz Schubert. Throughout my years in high school, I also played in two musicals, "All Shook Up" and "Once Upon A Mattress."

I currently reside in Long Beach, California where I'm enrolled in California State University, Long Beach as an undergraduate student. I am a music performance and education major in the Bob Cole Conservatory of Music. I play for the Cole Conservatory Symphony Orchestra. Past ensembles I have played for were the Cole Conservatory Wind Symphony,Symphonic Band, and New Music Ensemble. I gig around the Southern California area and currently study with Tom Peters. Teachers I've studied with in my years of college include Gary Karr and Dennis Trembly. I have coached bass sections for the Bob Cole Honors Festival and for Long Beach Polytechnic High School. I work in the Daniel Recital Hall as a stage and sound technician on the campus of California State University, Long Beach. I have work several types of performances and various organizational events. I also intern in the Carpenter Performing Arts Center and The Compound Studio in Signal Hill, California. I am experienced with running sound, setting up as stage help, and working as a lighting techinican.
